Game Rules

Goal of the game
In the classic UNO game, the goal is to be the first player to score 500 points. Each round, the player earns points by being the first to play all their cards from their hand. Points are awarded for all the cards remaining in the hands of the other players.

Game preparation
The cards are shuffled, and each player is dealt 7 cards, which they take into their hand. The remaining cards are placed face down in the middle to form the draw pile. The top card from the draw pile is turned over and placed next to it. This stack becomes the discard pile. A player is randomly selected to start the round.

Progression of the game
The first player places a card from their hand on the discard pile. The rule is: a card can only be placed on a card of the same color or the same number. The black cards are special action cards with specific rules (see Action Cards). If a player cannot play a matching card, they must draw a penalty card from the face-down pile. They can immediately play this card if it matches. If they don't have a matching card, the next player takes their turn. When a player has only one card left, they must say "UNO!" (which means "One") to signal that they have only one card left in their hand. If a player forgets to do this and another player notices before the next player draws or discards a card, the forgetful player must draw 1 penalty card. The round is won by the player who plays the last card. The points are tallied, and a new round is played.

Action Cards
In the game, there are black action cards with different functions, which are explained below.

Draw Two Cards

When this card is played, the next player must draw 2 cards and is not allowed to play any cards during their turn. This card can only be placed on a card of the corresponding color or on another "Draw Two Cards". If it's the first card played at the beginning of the game, the same rules apply.

Reverse Card

When this card is played, the direction of play changes. If the game was previously going clockwise, it will now go counterclockwise, and vice versa. This card can only be placed on a card of the corresponding color or on another Reverse Card. If this card is drawn at the beginning of the game, the dealer starts, and then the player to their right continues the game instead of the player to their left.

Skip Card

After this card is played, the next player is "skipped," meaning they lose their turn. This card can only be placed on a card of the same color or on another Skip Card. If this card is drawn at the beginning of the game, the player to the left of the dealer is "skipped," and the player to the left of that player continues the game.

Wild Card

The player who plays this card gets to decide which color should be played next. They can even choose the same color that is already in play. A Wild Card can also be played when the player has another legal card to play. If a Wild Card is drawn at the beginning of the game, the player to the left of the dealer gets to choose the color for the next play.

Wild Draw Four Card

This card is the most powerful one. The player who plays it gets to choose the color that continues the game, and the next player must draw 4 cards from the draw pile and forfeit their turn. Unfortunately, this card can only be played if the player holding it doesn't have a card in their hand that matches the color and value on the discard pile. Also, this card may not be played on top of another +4 Card or a wildcard. If this card is drawn at the beginning of the game, it should be returned to the pile, and another card should be drawn.

Penalty Cards

When a player does not follow the rules, they must draw one or more penalty cards. The penalty rules are as follows:
  • In UNO, if a player forgets to call "UNO!" after playing their second-to-last card and the next player has not yet played a card, they must draw two penalty cards. This is to encourage players to announce "UNO!" when they have only one card left to prevent others from catching the mistake.
  • In UNO, if a player plays a card when it's not their turn or plays the wrong card, they must take the card back and draw an additional penalty card. This rule is in place to ensure that players take their turns in the correct order and play valid cards.
  • In UNO, the +4 card can only be played when a player cannot follow the current color, except for other action cards. If the player targeted by the +4 card believes it was played incorrectly, they can challenge the previous player. The previous player must then prove that they couldn't correctly follow the color by showing their cards. If they can confirm this, the challenging player must draw 6 cards instead of the usual 4. However, if the previous player is found to have played the +4 card incorrectly, they themselves must draw the 4 cards as a penalty. This rule is in place to prevent the abuse of the +4 card.

Points
The player who is the first to discard all their cards receives the following points for the cards still held by their fellow players:

All numbered cards: Value
Draw Two cards 20 Points
Reverse card 20 Points
Skip Card 20 Points
Wild Card 50 Points
Wild Draw Four cards 50 Points

The player who reaches 500 points first wins the game. In addition to the standard rules of UNO, there are many modifications and variants.
